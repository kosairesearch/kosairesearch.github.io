Business

Paratech, established in 1973 and listed on KOSDAQ since 1997, is a specialized fire safety equipment company with a history as the first domestic developer, manufacturer, and certified producer of sprinklers in Korea. Its business spans two main segments: Manufacturing and Construction/Installation. The Manufacturing segment produces key fire safety components including sprinkler heads (K80, K115, K160 series), flow control valves, flexible sprinkler hoses, CPVC pipes, and residential kitchen fire suppression systems. The Construction segment covers the design, installation, supervision, and inspection of fire suppression and mechanical systems, serving residential buildings as well as semiconductor and display cleanrooms and other high-tech plant facilities. In 2025, the standalone manufacturing segment generated approximately KRW 67.8B in revenue, with the remainder attributable to the installation segment. On the export side, Paratech leverages the K160 large-volume sprinkler—the first in Asia and fifth globally to receive FM certification—to address markets in the Middle East, Southeast Asia, India, and North America. FM/UL/ULC-certified products can be deployed globally without local re-certification, a key competitive advantage with international buyers. Overseas sales reached KRW 7.9B in 2025 (+12.86% YoY), still a small share of consolidated revenue, but the company is actively expanding through large India and Middle East contracts. The company employs approximately 274 staff and operates its headquarters and main production facilities in Seosan, South Chungcheong Province.

Industry

The domestic fire suppression equipment market is tightly correlated with construction activity; declining housing starts and a broad construction-sector slowdown directly suppress installation order volumes. Investment cycles in advanced manufacturing plants—particularly semiconductor and display fabs—are a critical driver of cleanroom fire suppression contracts, making major chipmakers' capex decisions an important revenue variable. Internationally, the market is expanding as emerging economies industrialize and governments tighten fire safety regulations. India's policy-mandated fire safety code enhancements are generating large-scale procurement demand, while construction and infrastructure booms in the Middle East and Southeast Asia are expanding fire protection market size. Competitively, Chinese low-cost producers exert price pressure in emerging markets, but FM/UL/ULC certifications serve as meaningful barriers by enabling product deployment without local re-approval. Domestically, most fire equipment players combine manufacturing and installation, creating complex margin structures, while raw material prices and currency fluctuations further influence profitability.
