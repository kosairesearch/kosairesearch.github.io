Business

Founded in 1977, Douzone Bizon is Korea's leading enterprise software (ERP and business platform) firm, segmenting its product line into Lite, Standard, and Extended ERP by customer size. Flagship products include the 'ERP10' solution, the 'WEHAGO' cloud business platform, the 'Amaranth10' all-in-one digital business platform, and 'OmniEsol,' a next-generation integrated platform infused with AI. About 110,000 firms use its small-business Lite ERP, while roughly 20,000 firms use its mid-market Standard ERP and large-enterprise Extended ERP , giving it a broad customer base. On a nine-month cumulative basis, strategic cloud products accounted for revenue shares of 20% for WEHAGO, 23% for Amaranth10, and 15% for OmniEsol , reflecting a rapid cloud shift. The 'ONE AI' productivity tool, launched in June 2024, is embedded in core workflows such as ERP and groupware and acts as a catalyst for cloud migration. Its business spans ERP, cloud services, platform services, e-finance services, mobile solutions, and groupware . The company has also pursued digital finance via a stake in Jeju Bank and overseas expansion through a Japanese subsidiary as growth pillars. It holds an estimated 80% share in the domestic SME and mid-market segment, while competing against foreign ERP vendors such as SAP and Oracle in the large-enterprise market . OmniEsol has emerged as a key weapon for attacking that large-enterprise and public sector, as well as overseas markets including Japan.

Earnings

For 2025, consolidated revenue of KRW 446.30bn, operating profit of KRW 127.73bn, and net profit attributable to owners of KRW 92.32bn all set records. The 2025 results showed revenue up 10.9%, operating profit up 45.0%, and net profit up 57.1% year over year , highlighting profit leverage that outpaced revenue growth. Operating margin climbed in steps from 15.0% in 2022 to 19.5% in 2023, 21.9% in 2024, and 28.6% in 2025, while ROE improved from 5.4% to 8.0%, 10.9%, and 15.0% over the same period. By quarter, 2025 revenue rose from KRW 98.6bn in Q1 to KRW 127.1bn in Q4, and operating profit accelerated from KRW 21.6bn to KRW 25.2bn, KRW 34.8bn, and KRW 46.1bn. The Q4 operating margin reached 36.3%, the highest quarterly level since 2012 . As the business-platform-centric product portfolio was completed, the profit structure stabilized, and generative-AI use steadily reduced outsourcing costs —the core driver of margin improvement. Amaranth10 revenue grew roughly 80% year over year , with the Standard ERP transition leading growth. In Q1 2026, revenue of KRW 116.56bn, operating profit of KRW 35.02bn, and net profit attributable to owners of KRW 23.26bn rose sharply from the year-earlier quarter (KRW 98.6bn / 21.6bn / 12.0bn), exceeding the market's earlier Q1 preview (revenue KRW 107.0bn / operating profit KRW 27.8bn). Operating cash flow also expanded steadily from KRW 77.3bn in 2022 to KRW 129.6bn in 2025, supporting earnings quality.

Industry

Korea's ERP market is in a structural shift, with subscription revenue rising as the cloud (SaaS) transition takes hold. As of 2023, Douzone held a 16.6% share of the domestic ERP market, ranking second behind SAP (20.5%), and unlike SAP—which centers on large enterprises and the public sector—it is strong in the SME segment. The global cloud ERP market is growing at roughly 11% annually over 2022–2027, far above traditional ERP's 3%. The company is accelerating its transition, including discontinuing on-premise versions of Lite ERP to offer cloud products only. On the AI front, it advances OmniEsol and ONE AI under an 'AX beyond DX' banner and is expanding consulting and implementation partnerships with EY Korea, Deloitte, and Lotte Innovate. Still, competition with global vendors such as SAP, Oracle, and Workday remains a challenge in the large-enterprise market. While there are de-rating concerns for global SaaS firms following the release of Anthropic's AI agent, some view the threat of AI replacing SaaS as relatively limited given Douzone's SME and mid-market customer base. End demand is tied to small-business conditions and corporate digital investment, leaving some cyclicality.

Outlook

The most decisive fact is that the company has entered the process of leaving the KOSPI. Global PE firm EQT signed a deal in November 2025 to acquire the control stake held by Chairman Kim Yong-woo and others, then launched a tender offer for all remaining shares in February 2026 to delist the company. EQT said it secured an unusually high stake of 90% in the first tender and 94% in the second. Subsequently, with the tender offer, conversion claims, and on-market purchases combined, EQT's stake reached about 95.66% on an ex-treasury basis, meeting the requirements to file for voluntary delisting. The board resolved on April 27 to pursue a cash-out comprehensive share exchange, with the exchange date set for June 30. On-market standing purchases are expected to continue through June 25, the trading day before the scheduled trading-halt date. On the business side, after going private, the company is expected to focus on an AI-centered global reorganization and expansion of OmniEsol into the large-enterprise and Japanese markets, free from short-term earnings pressure—though this will likely proceed outside the scope of public disclosure. Management changes, including the departure of platform-business head Song Ho-cheol, are also cited as variables.

Valuation

At this stage, valuation is driven by the deal structure rather than a conventional earnings multiple. The share price has effectively converged on the KRW 120,000 level—EQT's tender price and the cash payment under the comprehensive share exchange—so the delisting timeline, not ordinary volatility, governs the price.
