Business

CU Medical Systems, founded in 2001 and KOSDAQ-listed in 2011, is a specialized emergency medical device company. As Asia's first AED developer, it derives approximately 91% of revenue from AEDs, covering both consumer-grade and professional hospital product lines. Its flagship 'i-PAD CU-SPR' features world-first IP66/IP68 dust- and waterproof certification, enabling operation in extreme environments and underpinning overseas market share gains. Exports account for approximately 83% of total revenue across 80+ countries. The German subsidiary focuses on the European Public Access market, including supply to major UK supermarket chains, while the Japanese subsidiary holds a 3rd–4th-place market position in Japan through competitive bidding and procurement, reportedly growing at 15%+ annually. In November 2025, CU Medical acquired management control of KOSDAQ-listed Bistos (patient monitoring/vital sign devices) for approximately ₩22B, expanding its portfolio from Class 3 defibrillators to a full Class 1–3 medical device lineup. Bistos brings a 120-country distribution network and FDA/CE-certified products (incubators, patient monitors, etc.), expected to serve as a bridgehead for entry into U.S., Middle Eastern, and Latin American markets. Joint R&D targeting smart AEDs and AI monitoring devices, as well as expansion into veterinary medical devices and home healthcare, are also in the pipeline.
