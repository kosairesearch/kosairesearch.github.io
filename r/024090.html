Business

DCM was established in 1972 as Daelim Chemical Industrial Co. and listed on KOSPI under its current name in 1999, specializing in coated steel plates and industrial films. Since 1991, the company has focused on laminated color steel plates, supplying exterior materials for refrigerators, washing machines, and air conditioners; interior panels for ships; and construction and furniture-grade steel products. Revenue is dominated by laminated color steel plates (approximately 94%, split across high-gloss and standard grades), with industrial films and other items comprising the remainder. Core raw materials—flat steel coil—are procured directly from POSCO and other major domestic mills, while laminating films are sourced from LG Chem and multiple other suppliers. The Yangsan plant in South Gyeongsang Province is geographically proximate to POSCO's production base and major export ports, providing a structural advantage in logistics costs and delivery responsiveness. Principal markets include domestic Korea alongside China, Southeast Asia, India, and Turkey, with overseas revenues consistently exceeding 80% of total sales. The high-gloss steel plate segment claims the world's largest production volume, supporting long-term supply relationships with global appliance and shipbuilding OEM clients. The company is actively expanding into composite panel manufacturing and deploying automation upgrades as part of its diversification and ESG strategy. Competitors include POSCO Steeleon, TCC Steel, and SEAH CM; DCM is ranked approximately fifth in the surface-treated steel sector by revenue.
