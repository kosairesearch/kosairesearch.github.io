Business

GeneMatrix was founded in 2000 at the Seoul National University College of Medicine's cancer research institute and listed on KOSDAQ in 2009. Its core business is the development and sale of molecular diagnostic reagents and instruments, underpinned by proprietary technologies including C-Tag, RFMP, Encleap, TriO, and MQ-TeQ. The flagship 'NeoPlex' product series enables multiplex simultaneous detection of sexually transmitted infections (STI), human papillomavirus (HPV), tuberculosis and non-tuberculous mycobacteria (TB/NTM), and respiratory pathogens in a single test. The biochemical diagnostics segment covers approximately 30 standard and 10 differentiated biochemical assays (including GOT and GPT), complemented by point-of-care testing (POCT) products. Beyond Korea, the company exports to the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia; European CE certification has recently been renewed and local partnerships in the Middle East and Southeast Asia are being expanded. Diagnostics form the primary revenue base, while a secondary R&D pipeline includes vaccines (hepatitis A, Japanese encephalitis, herpes zoster) and an AI-designed gene delivery platform 'GM-CAD1' targeting immuno-oncology. The drug pipeline is at the preclinical-to-Phase 1 IND stage, with peer-reviewed efficacy data published for 'GM-CAD1-P1' in a cervical cancer model and 'GM-CAD1-Z1' in a herpes zoster model.
