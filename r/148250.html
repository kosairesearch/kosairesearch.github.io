Business

RN2 Technologies, founded in 2002, is a LTCC (Low Temperature Co-fired Ceramics)-based electronic components manufacturer headquartered in Hwaseong, Gyeonggi Province, with additional production facilities in Gangneung. Its core MLC (Multi-Layer Components) segment produces ceramic passive components—including RF couplers, chip antennas, delay lines, and terminations—for 5G base stations and repeaters, accounting for the majority of revenues. The company holds the position of global No.2 supplier of couplers for base station repeaters, supplying all five major global telecom equipment manufacturers: Samsung Electronics, Ericsson, Huawei, Nokia, and ZTE. A vertically integrated production system—from LTCC powder to finished product—underpins its cost competitiveness. The MCP (Multi-Layer Ceramic PCB) segment supplies multilayer ceramic substrates for defense and aerospace applications, while the Material segment sells LTCC powder externally. The company is also expanding into automotive electronics, including supply of couplers for EV inertial navigation systems in China, broadening its application base beyond traditional telecom. Through its subsidiary RN2 Ceramics, it is developing ceramic heat dissipation substrates for EV power semiconductor packaging as a new growth engine, holding four registered core structural patents and nine additional filed patents. Fourteen registered circuit-design patents and superior high-frequency and environmental-resistance characteristics constitute the primary technical moat.
