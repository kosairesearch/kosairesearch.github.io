Business

Samhyun Steel was established in 1978, incorporated in 1984, and listed on the KOSDAQ in 2001 as a steel distribution and processing specialist anchored in the Yeongnam region of South Korea. Its core business rests on two authorized dealer contracts: as a POSCO-authorized hot-rolled steel distributor and processing center, it purchases, processes, and sells hot-rolled sheet products including thin-gauge, heavy-gauge, patterned, and pickled grades; and as a Hyundai Steel-authorized structural steel dealer, it handles a wide range of structural products such as H-beams, I-beams, deformed bars, and various pipe and tube products. At its Gwangyang Factory 1 and Factory 2, the company operates heavy-gauge and light-gauge slitting lines, as well as marking and punching lines, enabling customized cut-to-order, bending, drilling, and marking services under a Total Steel Service model. End-market demand comes primarily from construction, shipbuilding, automotive, and general machinery industries. Because profitability depends on the spread between steel product selling prices and raw material procurement costs from POSCO and Hyundai Steel, steel market conditions are the most critical earnings driver. The company's status as an authorized distributor for Korea's two largest steelmakers acts as a meaningful barrier to entry. Total shares issued stand at approximately 15.7 million, with around 260,000 held as treasury shares, placing the company in the KOSDAQ small-cap category.

Earnings

After reaching a peak with revenue of KRW 340.6B and operating profit of KRW 19.1B (OPM 5.6%) in FY2022, Samhyun Steel experienced a sharp deterioration in profitability as steel prices fell and end-market demand weakened. Revenue declined to KRW 262.4B (OPM 3.7%) in FY2023 and KRW 223.7B (OPM 1.6%) in FY2024, pressured by low-cost import competition and sluggish construction activity. FY2025 saw revenue edge slightly lower to KRW 218.0B, but operating profit recovered to KRW 5.1B (OPM 2.3%) as anti-dumping tariffs reduced import volumes and cost-efficiency efforts took hold. At the quarterly level, operating profit in 2025Q1 was a near-breakeven KRW 138M, but improved progressively to KRW 1.87B in Q2, KRW 1.0B in Q3, and KRW 2.09B in Q4. In 2026Q1, revenue reached KRW 57.4B with operating profit of KRW 1.54B—a dramatic improvement from the near-zero level of 2025Q1—confirming that the better pricing environment is flowing through to results. Operating cash flow remained above reported net income at KRW 6.8B in FY2025 (KRW 9.2B in FY2024, KRW 15.9B in FY2023), reflecting healthy cash generation throughout the cycle. The debt ratio has declined continuously from 18.4% in FY2022 to 10.4% in FY2025, with equity of KRW 197B providing a robust balance-sheet foundation.

Risk factors

The longer the lead time between procurement and sale, the larger the inventory exposure to price-decline risk. In periods when raw material prices rise faster than selling prices—which can occur before end-demand fully recovers—input cost inflation may outpace the ability to pass through price increases.
  • Simultaneous Demand Weakness Across Construction, Shipbuilding, and Automotive: If construction, shipbuilding, and automotive—Samhyun's key downstream markets—enter a simultaneous downturn, volume shock could materialize across all segments, limiting the benefit of end-market diversification. A scenario in which the construction slump extends further and shipbuilding order cycles also turn down could drive incremental revenue declines.   • Indirect Exposure to Escalating Global Protectionism: The escalation of global protectionism—including the US 50% tariff on steel imports and tightening EU safeguards—is eroding the export profitability of Korean steelmakers such as POSCO and Hyundai Steel. As these companies divert export volumes to the domestic market, domestic oversupply concerns could intensify, putting further pressure on distribution and processing margins. Although Samhyun has virtually no direct export exposure, it remains indirectly vulnerable to structural shifts in the domestic supply chain.
