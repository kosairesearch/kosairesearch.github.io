Business

Noul Inc. was founded in 2015 and listed on KOSDAQ under a technology-based special listing exception in 2022, operating as a medical AI and bioengineering diagnostics company. Its core offering, the miLab™ on-device AI platform, automates the full diagnostic workflow—sample preparation (smearing, staining), digital imaging, and AI analysis—within a single compact device. The cloud-free, offline-capable design gives Noul a differentiated edge in settings with limited IT infrastructure, particularly mid-sized and small diagnostic laboratories in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). The company currently offers three solution lines: miLab MAL (malaria), miLab BCM (blood analysis/CBC), and miLab CER (cervical cancer), enabling multi-disease diagnosis from one platform. The razor-and-blades business model combines device hardware sales with recurring consumable cartridge revenue, designed so that a growing installed base drives compounding cartridge subscription revenue over time. Reference clients include Korea's KDCA, Novartis, US Inova, Harvard Medical School/Massachusetts General Hospital, Seoul Asan Hospital, Germany's Charité Hospital, and the Limbach diagnostics chain. Sales geographies are diversifying across Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Uganda), Europe (Germany, Italy, Romania), the Middle East (Saudi Arabia), Southeast Asia (Indonesia), and Latin America (Mexico, Panama). The International Drug Purchase Facility (Unitaid) recognized miLab as the most advanced form of digital microscopy platform in its 2022 official report, and Noul competes against large IVD incumbents—Abbott, Roche, Siemens—as well as emerging AI-POC players such as Sight Diagnostics.
