Business

Founded in 1988, Mirae Asset Life is a life insurer whose business combines protection, variable and annuity products with asset-management fees (fee-biz). Insurance premium revenue is the core, complemented by separate-account, fee and financial (investment) income. The firm has adopted a fee-biz strategy centered on variable insurance and retirement pensions; in Q1 this year variable insurance fee income rose 15.7% year on year to KRW 14.3bn, while variable reserves grew 15.0% to KRW 13.3tn. Health insurance first-month premiums jumped 97% year on year, expanding its protection-market presence, while in variable insurance it kept the industry's top spot in both first premiums and cumulative premium income. For 2026 the company has set out a "lifetime income design" strategy, aiming to expand sales of guaranteed performance-linked IRP products and upgrade its fund lineup to precisely support customers' post-retirement cash-flow design. On ownership, Mirae Asset group affiliates hold large stakes, with Mirae Asset Securities at 22.01%, Mirae Asset Global Investments at 17.59%, Mirae Asset Capital at 15.59% and Mirae Asset Consulting at 5.21%. Competitively it faces large life insurers such as Samsung Life and Hanwha Life directly in the variable and health segments. Management has also framed a "Korean Berkshire Hathaway" model combining insurance and principal investment as its long-term vision.

Industry

The life insurance sector has been reshaped since the 2023 introduction of IFRS17, with fair-value liability measurement and CSM-centered revenue recognition. IFRS17 shifts liability valuation to fair value, which favors insurers with a low share of legacy high-rate fixed products and a focus on protection insurance. Still, conditions are challenging: the first rate cut after IFRS17 also weighs on life insurers, since falling rates sharply increase liabilities and worsen capital adequacy, especially for life insurers with large legacy high-rate savings books. In addition, the industry, after pursuing short-term gains via CSM accumulation under the new standard, now faces concerns over impaired financial soundness. Competitively, non-life insurers have traditionally dominated the third-sector (health and accident) market, while life insurers are still expanding their presence. Against this backdrop, Mirae Asset Life is differentiating through its top spot in variable insurance and expansion in health new business. The market views insurers that can expand shareholder-return policies such as buybacks and dividends as leading beneficiaries within the low price-to-book sector. Ultimately, rate direction, capital adequacy and the strength of shareholder returns are the variables driving stock-level differentiation.
