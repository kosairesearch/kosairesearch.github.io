Business

JNB (452160) was founded in 2002 and listed on KOSDAQ in November 2023 through a SPAC merger with DB Financial SPAC No. 9. Although officially categorized under the 'Automotive' DART industry code, the company's actual primary business centers on vacuum pump stacker systems and ultra-precision machined parts for semiconductor manufacturing facilities. The Stacker System — a utility platform integrating vibration absorption, noise suppression, and supply of power and cooling water to fab vacuum pumps — has served as a production-line standard at Samsung Electronics for over 20 years since JNB developed it in 2003. The ultra-precision parts segment employs 1μm-grade machining equipment and 0.1μm-level 3D measurement tools to produce components for ultra-high vacuum pumps. Samsung Electronics is effectively the sole customer; JNB completed the design and manufacture of stacker systems for the 14nm/28nm Samsung Austin Semiconductor (SAS) fab in Texas and has been developing a next-generation stacker for Samsung's Taylor Fab S6 line (4nm process) since 2022, targeting SEMI-S2, UL certification, and Texas state regulatory compliance. Additional products include vacuum and pressure chambers and power distribution units. The company operates from its Pyeongtaek, Gyeonggi-do facility and holds ISO9001/ISO14001 and INNO-BIZ certifications.
