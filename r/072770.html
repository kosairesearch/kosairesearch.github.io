Business

MemRAY BT (formerly Yulho, 072770) is a KOSDAQ-listed IT solutions company established in 2007 that primarily supplies enterprise server and storage solutions. Revenue is almost entirely derived from Dell Technologies servers, storage, and software, with the company holding Dell's highest partner tier—Titanium Tier 1—in Korea. Key subsidiaries include manufacturing arm The Stellar, and the company also operates QUEST solution services. Major customers include financial institutions, public-sector organizations, and large corporates; press reports indicate a ₩18.6bn IT equipment integration contract with NH Bank, and a reported ₩50.0bn supercomputer parts supply deal with Samsung Electronics in 2024. In December 2025, the company changed its name to MemRAY BT at an extraordinary general meeting, formally embedding its strategic alliance with domestic semiconductor fabless startup Memray into its corporate identity. MemRAY BT secured an exclusive license for Memray's ByteFlash patents—technology designed to dramatically expand GPU-accessible memory by treating flash storage as DRAM—in December 2025. Memray holds a total of 34 technology patents, with three domestic and three U.S. patents specifically covering ByteFlash. The company is executing a dual strategy: maintaining its existing Dell IT reselling base while layering AI semiconductor IP licensing and joint product development on top.
