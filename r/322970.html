Business

Founded in 2016, Moogene Medi has grown as a nano-medicine specialist whose core competency lies in its LNP drug-delivery platform. The company can load gene-editing materials (CRISPR/Cas9), protein therapeutics, and mRNA onto LNPs for targeted in-vivo delivery, and holds proprietary LNP composition patents that circumvent those held by global pharma majors. Its business is structured around two pillars: the cosmetic raw materials and dermatological devices segment, which contributed approximately 73% of revenues in FY2024, and the LNP-based pharmaceutical CDMO segment, which accounted for roughly 27%. The R&D pipeline spans the colorectal cancer candidate DREC-21, the pancreatic cancer candidate PDAC-11, the topical hair-loss therapy HUTERA, a type-2 diabetes treatment, an mRNA cancer vaccine, and rare-disease therapeutics. HUTERA is differentiated from oral formulations by its nano-liposome microbubb structure containing finasteride or dutasteride, enabling follicle-cell delivery through simple scalp application. In terms of competition, domestic LNP players include Yuhan, Enhanced Bio, and Inventage Lab, while globally Moderna, Pfizer/BioNTech dominate LNP know-how, leaving intense competitive pressure. Moogene Medi offsets its small scale via proprietary composition patents and an established GMP facility as a CDMO base. Headquartered in Pangyo, Seongnam, the company operates with a staff in the mid-30s, reflecting its research-focused, small-enterprise character.
