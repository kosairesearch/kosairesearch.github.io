Business

Justec was established in 1999 through KIST's venture development program and holds the distinction of being Korea's first domestic developer of linear motors, forming the technological foundation for its vertically integrated ultra-precision motion control solutions; the company is headquartered in Pyeongtaek, Gyeonggi Province. It operates under a co-CEO structure with Kim Yong-il and Choi Dong-su, listed in KOSDAQ's Technology Growth Enterprise segment. Its core product portfolio spans four verticals: semiconductor motion systems, display motion systems, industrial automation motion systems, and space motion systems. The key competitive differentiator is a vertically integrated structure in which the company self-develops critical components — linear motors, direct-drive (DD) motors, drivers, and motion controllers — and integrates them into LMS (linear motion systems), precision stages, and specialty motion systems, providing superior cost control and customization capabilities compared to competitors reliant on external component procurement. Proprietary technologies in vibration suppression, friction reduction, and thermal expansion control underpin sub-nanometer (1 nm) positional accuracy, supported by 49 patents in ultra-precision motion control. In FY2025, revenue was distributed across semiconductor (41.8%; KRW 8.9 billion), display (approximately 45%), industrial automation (5.6%), and space (0.84%); however, in Q1 2026 the semiconductor share expanded to approximately 57% while display contracted to 21.8%, reflecting an accelerating structural shift. In the semiconductor space, Justec supplies motion stages on an exclusive basis to domestic equipment makers Iotechnicis and DIT, with Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, and Micron as end customers — specifically for laser cutting, annealing, and die bonding processes in HBM manufacturing. In aerospace, the company has participated in satellite attitude control device development — Reaction Wheel Assemblies (RWA) and Control Moment Gyroscopes (CMG) — with KARI since 2012, accumulating in-orbit verification credentials through programs including the Nuri launch vehicle performance verification satellite. The company maintains commercial relationships with over 100 customers, many spanning more than a decade, and Japan accounted for approximately 18.3% of total FY2025 revenues, with global expansion anchored by a local subsidiary.
