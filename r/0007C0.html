Business

Acryl was founded in 2011 by CEO Park Wae-jin, a KAIST computer science PhD, and is positioned as a full-stack AI transformation (AX) infrastructure company supporting AI service deployment and operations end-to-end; the company went public on KOSDAQ via a technology special listing in December 2025. Its two flagship product lines are the integrated AI platform 'Jonathan' and the healthcare-focused AI platform 'NADIA.' Jonathan is an end-to-end platform covering data preprocessing and labeling, model training and deployment, MLOps/LLMOps, validation, and retraining, reportedly reducing AI development costs and timelines by over 70% through GPU resource optimization. Revenue mix in 2024 comprised Jonathan at 46.8%, government information subsidies at 27.1%, consulting services at 18.0%, and NADIA at 8.1%, meaning platform revenue and public subsidies account for roughly three-quarters of total sales. NADIA is a healthcare AI platform for medical data integration and AI-assisted diagnosis, with overseas installations at public hospitals in Uzbekistan and an AI-native EMR product (NADIA-ANE) in active development. The company's GPUBASE GPU cluster optimization software is being nurtured as a new growth engine, and its proprietary LLM 'Areum (A-LLM)' ranked first in the domestic open-source LLM leaderboard in August 2024. Key customers include Samsung E&A, Samsung Welstory, and Seegene, totaling approximately 80 clients, while strategic investors LG Electronics and SK C&C provide access to major conglomerate ecosystems. Domestic competitors include Upstage, Nota, KeyTalk AI, and Aillus, but Acryl's combination of GPU infrastructure optimization, public sector track record, and specialized healthcare platform constitutes a meaningful differentiation. With approximately 90 employees and over 50 patents, the company has earned recognition including Forbes Korea 'AI 50' for two consecutive years.
