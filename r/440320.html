Business

Founded in 2012 and listed on KOSDAQ via a technology-exception IPO in 2023, OpenKnowl is an AI-based career and recruitment platform company. Its flagship service, 'Mini-Intern,' connects job seekers with companies through online and offline practical-work matching, enabling candidates to complete short internships and verify job competencies, with the business expanding into AI-driven AX (AI Transformation) education and experienced-hire matching. The company is enhancing skill-up systems, display portfolios, HR SaaS, and AI/VR capabilities. In 2024, it acquired a 58.4% stake in Hyundai IT—Korea's No. 2 digital whiteboard supplier—for approximately KRW 15 billion, adding digital signage and smart boards to its portfolio. Hyundai IT serves commercial venues, corporate clients, and educational institutions while exporting products to over 30 countries including the U.S., France, and Japan. Based on 2025 consolidated revenue, the segment mix was: platform ~KRW 28.5 billion (32%), application software ~KRW 8.6 billion (10%), and digital signage ~KRW 51.5 billion (58%), with hardware-infrastructure now dominating the revenue base (per Platum, February 2026). The company is pursuing an integrated online-offline career platform strategy using the digital signage infrastructure as a distribution channel for AX education content, and participates as a private operator in government-sponsored youth employment programs—including selection for a 2026 Gyeonggi Province AI education consortium.

Earnings

FY2023 consolidated revenue of KRW 25.1 billion (OPM 5.4%) and net income of KRW 1.41 billion represented a healthy start, but the consolidation of Hyundai IT roughly doubled FY2024 revenue to KRW 50.8 billion (+102% YoY) while compressing the operating margin to just 0.2%, highlighting a clear growth-versus-profitability trade-off. FY2025 set an all-time revenue record at KRW 88.3 billion (+74% YoY), but produced a first-ever annual operating loss of KRW –2.8 billion (OPM –3.1%) and a controlling-interest net loss of KRW –6.5 billion. On a quarterly basis, Q1 (+KRW 0.52 billion OP) and Q3 (+KRW 1.29 billion OP) were profitable, while Q2 (–KRW 1.51 billion) and Q4 (–KRW 3.08 billion) were loss-making, revealing pronounced seasonality and volatility. Crucially, FY2025 operating cash flow (CFO) turned positive at +KRW 1.91 billion, indicating core business cash generation remained intact despite headline losses. Q1 2026 recorded operating income of KRW 1.0 billion (OPM ~4.5%) and controlling-interest net income of KRW 0.55 billion, marking a return to quarterly profitability. The debt ratio surged from 21.4% (pre-acquisition 2023) to 89.3% (2025), and the current ratio fell from 833.8% to 84.6% over the same period, elevating short-term liquidity management as a near-term priority.
