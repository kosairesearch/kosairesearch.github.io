Risk factors

  • End-market demand cycle: Earnings are highly geared to AI data-center and grid investment, so a slowdown or delay in big-tech capex would inevitably hit orders and revenue. If the power-infrastructure supercycle passes its peak, the high-margin structure could weaken. The cycle's position needs continuous monitoring.
  • Raw materials and FX: Prices of raw materials—copper for cables, precious metals and sulfuric acid for smelting—and FX moves directly affect margins. LS MnM faces copper-smelting profitability concerns from falling treatment charges, but precious-metal and sulfuric-acid byproduct profits offset this. If the byproduct price cycle turns, earnings volatility could rise.

Room for a narrower NAV discount from possible government-led reform of dual subsidiary listings is cited, but not yet fully reflected. Valuation may hinge on the direction of regulation and policy.
