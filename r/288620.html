Business

S-Prism (formerly S-Fuelcell; KOSDAQ 288620) was established in 2014 and listed in 2018 as a first-generation Korean PEMFC (Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cell) specialist with fuel cell business roots dating to 2001. Its core product is LNG-reformed building-use fuel cell systems ranging from 1 kW to 25 kW, with a cumulative installed capacity exceeding 15 MW. Beyond buildings, the company has expanded into power generation (200 kW-class systems and plant operations) and mobility powerpack applications for electric trucks and hydrogen forklifts, supported by vertically integrated design capability across stacks, reformers, and power conversion systems—backed by 21 domestic and international patents and 13 government R&D projects. The April 2026 rebrand introduced the GFOS (Grid-Free, On-Site) platform as the company's primary strategic initiative, pairing steady baseload from building-use FCs with fast-responding PEMFC powerpacks for peak shaving, scalable via parallel modules to up to 1 MW per site. S-Prism operates as the hydrogen fuel cell arm of its parent, S-Energy—Korea's first solar module maker—alongside subsidiary S-Power in O&M. Revenue is most heavily weighted toward building-use fuel cells, with government subsidy dependence and natural gas input cost sensitivity as structural drivers of earnings volatility.
