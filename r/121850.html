Business

Founded in 2006 and listed on KOSDAQ in September 2012, KOYJ (Coyj Co., Ltd.) is a specialty optical materials company for LCD displays. Its core business is the manufacture and sale of optical films used in LCD backlighting units (BLU), including prism coating films, light guide panels (LGP), and diffusion films, with a subsidiary established in Nanjing, China in 2007 to supply the Asian market. The main revenue stream relied on Chinese partner networks to deliver to LCD panel manufacturers; however, in 2025 the divestiture of a key Chinese partner's business unit eliminated a critical customer channel. In pursuit of business diversification, the company is developing OLED anti-reflection films, Mini LED lens optical materials, and commercializing aluminum oxide (Al2O3) as an additive for secondary battery cathode materials based on nano-metal oxide technology licensed from KAIST. Al2O3 targets the NCA and NCMA cathode additive market—currently dominated by a small number of global suppliers with limited domestic Korean alternatives—though commercial revenues have yet to materialize. The competitive landscape is structurally challenging: vertical integration by China's major panel makers (BOE, CSOT) and aggressive low-price strategies by Chinese optical film manufacturers continue to erode pricing power. The company has established subsidiaries for new business ventures since 2018 and operates development teams in the Seoul metropolitan area and Ulsan. No dividends have been paid, and following the 10:1 reverse stock split and rights offering, total outstanding shares stand at approximately 5.06 million.
