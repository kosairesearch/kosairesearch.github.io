Business

DTC was founded in 1998 for LCD module development, manufacturing, and sales, listing on KOSDAQ in 2002. Its legacy core business supplies small-to-medium LCD module display solutions for mobile devices and tablets, primarily through overseas outsourced manufacturing for Samsung Electronics. Although KRX classifies DTC under the 'Real Estate & REITs' sector, the real estate rental service functions only as a supplementary income stream, with the consolidated group's revenues predominantly driven by display and electronic component businesses. In November 2024, DTC acquired a 19.22% stake in Lumens for approximately KRW 26bn, becoming its largest shareholder in January 2025; by April 2026, the stake had expanded to approximately 21.97%. The Lumens consolidation added three new business segments—LED lighting and components, light guide panels (LGP), and automotive electronics—to the group's consolidated entity. Lumens has been a pioneer in medium-to-large LCD BLU LED markets since 2007, having developed the world's first Flip-Chip solution, Edge-type BLU, and Quantum Dot LED; current R&D focuses on next-generation MLD, MLP, and FLD technologies for Mini and Micro LED applications. DTC established an Indian subsidiary in 2019, and Lumens operates production facilities in Vietnam and China as part of the group's manufacturing network. Key downstream customers include Samsung Electronics and major global IT and consumer electronics firms, while Lumens has established a Micro LED supply relationship with Swedish defense company AIMPOINT.
