Business

Rokit Healthcare, founded in 2012 by Yoo Seok-hwan, a former Celltrion executive, listed on KOSDAQ in May 2025 as a regenerative-medicine specialist. Its core model combines bioink made from patient-derived extracellular matrix (ECM), AI wound modeling, and 3D bioprinting into a hyper-personalized organ-regeneration platform. It primarily targets complex chronic conditions such as diabetic foot, harvesting ECM from the patient's own fat and using bioprinting to produce a custom skin-regeneration patch within 30 minutes. Diabetic foot is the main driver of revenue growth. Beyond this, it is expanding into skin, cartilage, and kidney regeneration. The company developed an autologous ECM-based bioink kit, an auto-modeling AI application, and a 3D printer, securing FDA, CE MDR and medical certifications in 14 countries plus commercialization sales contracts in 46 countries. Consolidated subsidiaries include Rokit America (100%), which handles NMN- and fisetin-based health supplements, and single-cell RNA analysis firm Rokit Genomics (88.3%). Revenue is a blended mix of devices, single-use consumable kits, and treatment services, with kits used repeatedly per patient and a focus on high gross margins. Overseas expansion across the US, Middle East, and Israel is a key growth axis.

Industry

Regenerative medicine is a growth end-market driven by aging populations and rising chronic disease. It is seen as a core medical technology of the aging era, with the global market posting double-digit annual growth, and tissue/organ restoration regarded as a next-generation solution addressing both transplant demand and immune rejection. Diabetic foot, Rokit's main target, has a large patient pool and high recurrence and amputation risk, creating repeat treatment demand. Cartilage, once damaged, rarely heals naturally, and conventional microfracture tends to form fibrocartilage rather than the normal hyaline cartilage, limiting durability and raising re-injury risk. The company seeks differentiation by addressing this unmet need with single-session regeneration. Still, alternatives such as stem-cell therapies, existing wound-care devices, and orthopedic surgery compete, and insurance reimbursement listing governs adoption speed. In the US, diabetic-foot regeneration at a major tertiary hospital was recognized for public reimbursement and classified as a CPT-coded procedure, and Israel's largest public insurer approved a pilot study toward listing. Government-led investment expansion in the Middle East healthcare market is also cited as a potential opportunity.

Outlook

The company frames indication expansion as its medium-to-long-term growth engine. Having obtained advanced regenerative-medicine clinical approval, Rokit will begin first-in-human application of autologous omentum-derived cell kidney regeneration in chronic kidney disease patients in July 2026, aiming to confirm early safety and regenerative potential. It is also running a first-of-its-kind AI-based cartilage-regeneration global trial, validating efficacy and safety via randomized studies in knee cartilage-defect patients across Korea, the US, and South America. It plans to extend the domestic cartilage trial into the Middle East through a partner. The company plans roughly KRW 15bn of R&D and clinical investment in 2026, allocating about KRW 5bn each to cartilage and kidney regeneration and KRW 2.5bn to skin. To fund this, it approved a roughly KRW 62.5bn rights offering in March 2026. In the US it has accumulated over 1,100 real-world treatment data cases and is expanding cooperation with Northwell Health, one of the East Coast's largest medical networks. However, such clinical and expansion spending may weigh on near-term profitability, making the balance between top-line growth and cost execution decisive.
