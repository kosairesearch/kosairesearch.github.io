Business

Sebang is the parent holding company of Sebang Group, established in 1965 for port stevedoring. Its core business spans a full suite of integrated logistics services — import/export container and bulk cargo stevedoring, warehousing, land and sea transportation, heavy-lift transport and installation, and 3PL — operating from 13 nationwide port hubs and handling approximately 42 million tonnes of bulk cargo annually. The container segment handles transshipment, consolidation, and last-mile delivery via CY/CFS facilities, backed by a multimodal (road, rail, sea) transport network. The heavy-cargo segment serves industrial plant projects in refining, chemicals, and power. Growth businesses include cold chain logistics (10 nationwide centers; key clients: Costco, E-Mart, Homeplus, Samyang, and Maeil Dairy), e-commerce fulfillment (B2C parcel processing), and solar power generation. Overseas, the company operates in Vietnam (SEBANG VINA) and Belgium, with the Belgian subsidiary handling international EV battery module logistics and warehousing for affiliates Sebang Global Battery and Sebang Lithium Battery. The consolidated group includes 38 subsidiaries, with listed affiliate Sebang Global Battery (004490) being the most strategically significant for equity value and dividend income. Competition is multi-layered, with CJ Logistics, Hanjin, Dongbang, and Interworks as primary rivals across port, transport, and 3PL segments, while nationwide infrastructure scale serves as the primary barrier to entry.
