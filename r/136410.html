Business

ASSEMS entered the eco-friendly adhesive materials market in 2003 by developing the world's first release-liner-free hot melt adhesive film, and listed on KOSDAQ in 2022. Its core product is a solvent-free hot melt film adhesive that enables the transition from conventional solvent-based and sewing processes to seamless, automated production lines. As of 1Q25, revenue by end-market comprised footwear (68.4%), automotive (29.5%), and other (2.1%); products are supplied through ODM/OEM manufacturers to global sports brands including Nike, Adidas, and New Balance. In the automotive segment, the company supplies panoramic sunroof fabric to Hyundai and Kia. R&D is concentrated at the Busan headquarters, while overseas subsidiaries in Vietnam and Indonesia provide cost-competitive manufacturing under a vertically integrated model. New product initiatives include the mesh-structured Ultranet adhesive, anti-fungal adhesive film, anhydrous dye yarn, and high-value display and medical-grade adhesive materials. The company's vertically integrated model—extending from materials through bonding machinery—creates meaningful customer switching costs and a durable competitive moat. The strong alignment of its eco-friendly product profile with global brands' carbon-reduction mandates provides a structural tailwind for new product adoption.
