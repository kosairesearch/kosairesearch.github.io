Business

Sol Defense (formerly Husenstech, before that EDT and Unimax Global) was founded in 1999 and adopted its current name in April 2025, reflecting a strategic refocus on defense electronics. The business spans four areas: avionics control and display systems, guided-weapon control, tactical command and control, and civilian applications such as power-plant and railway control boards. The avionics segment supplies critical electronics to a wide range of Korean military platforms, including the FA-50, T-50, KF-21, LAH small armed helicopter, MAH marine assault helicopter, and MCH mine-countermeasure helicopter. For the KF-21 program, Sol Defense provides mission computers — integrating flight control, weapon control, navigation, and sensor-data processing — and high-resolution multi-function displays (MFD) running on multi-core CPUs with the VxWorks-653 RTOS. The guided-weapon segment develops and produces control units such as the NGU and GCU for tactical multiple-launch rocket systems. Principal customers are Hanwha Systems and Hanwha Aerospace, placing Sol Defense at the first-tier supplier level within the Korean defense supply chain. Quality accreditations including Hanwha Systems QAP certification and Hanwha Thales special-process certification reinforce its supply-chain position. As of May 2026, the company employs approximately 107 people, with engineers comprising over 20% of the workforce.
