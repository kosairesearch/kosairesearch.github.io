Business

ICTK, established in 2017, is a fabless security semiconductor design firm recognized as the world's first commercializer of Physically Unclonable Function (PUF)-based security chips. Its proprietary VIA PUF technology exploits manufacturing variation in via-hole formation to assign each chip a unique digital fingerprint, enabling a hardware Root of Trust without any externally stored security key. The company was listed on KOSDAQ in May 2024 under the technology-growth special track. Its product portfolio spans three pillars: security chips (G3, G5N, G5Q, STR, MTB series), security modules and devices, and solution development services including IP licensing; based on 2023 IPO disclosure data, revenue was split approximately 44.6% security chips, 14.0% security modules and devices, and 41.3% solution development services. Chips are designed in-house and fabricated by Samsung Foundry, SK Keyfoundry, and Taiwan's UMC, with back-end processing performed in a cleanroom at the Gangnam headquarters—an asset-light fabless model. Key customers include LG U+ (USIM, eSIM, Wi-Fi routers, CCTV), Korea Electric Power (smart meters), and Chinese smart-card and door-lock markets; mass supply to a Silicon Valley global big-tech client was officially announced in May 2026. Competitive pressure is intensifying as Synopsys (which acquired Intrinsic ID), Rambus, and Infineon accelerate PUF-based Hardware Root of Trust (HRoT) commercialization globally. Domestically, Dream Security, KCS, and Xgate compete in adjacent quantum-security niches. ICTK has built its public-sector foundation through contributions to the ISO/IEC 20897 international standard, recognition as a Root-of-Trust leader in the GSA IoT Security working group, and NIS KCMVP Hardware Level 2 certification.
