Business

Pungkang was founded in 1974 in Hwaseong, Gyeonggi Province, specializing in automotive nut and bolt manufacturing, and listed on KOSDAQ in 2007. Its core products are cold-forged automotive fasteners—primarily Weld Nuts, Hex Nuts, Flange Nuts, and Wheel Nuts—backed by over 40 years of accumulated manufacturing expertise and the IATF 16949 quality management system to meet stringent OEM quality requirements. The customer base spans major Korean OEMs including Hyundai/Kia, GM Korea, Renault Korea, and KG Mobility, as well as their Tier-1 suppliers, with exports also supplying automotive lines in Japan, China, and the United States. In response to the EV transition, the company has been expanding into battery component parts via LG Chem Tier-1 partners, broadening its product portfolio toward EV-related items. CEO Kim Jin-yong (Ph.D., Seoul National University) serves as both chief executive and largest shareholder, pursuing an owner-operator model with a strategy of expanding product coverage through value-engineering proposals and proactive new-model development responses. Subsidiary PK Mechatronics (50% stake) operates in industrial machinery manufacturing and sales, providing some revenue base outside automotive. No dilutive financing instruments (rights issues or convertible bonds) have been issued in recent years, and the company maintains a conservative financial posture funded largely by internal cash flow generation.

Operating cash flow remained robust at KRW 7.0 bn in 2025, materially exceeding net income and confirming solid underlying cash generation capacity. Across the four-year span, revenue has declined for two consecutive years since the 2023 peak, and OPM has collapsed from 5.4% to 0.8%—a structural margin compression that remains the central constraint on earnings-based valuation re-rating.
