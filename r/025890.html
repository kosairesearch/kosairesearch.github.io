Business

Hankook Steel Co., Ltd. was established in Haman, South Gyeongsang Province in 1987 and listed on KOSPI in 1997. Its business comprises two pillars. The first is the manufacture of large cast steel products for shipbuilding, power generation, defense, and industrial plant applications; key products include turbine casings, butterfly valves, gate valves, and stern castings supplied as core materials to shipbuilders and power or industrial plant operators. The second is the compressed scrap iron business, in which waste iron is collected and compacted for full supply to electric arc furnace steelmakers at approximately 470 tonnes per day average output, with a production line expansion under review. Major customers include Hanwha Ocean, Samsung Heavy Industries (shipbuilding), and Doosan Enerbility (power generation), with additional orders from Samsung Heavy Industries' FPSO program and Doosan Enerbility turbine casings anticipated. The large cast steel market has high barriers to entry due to substantial upfront capital investment and lengthy technology development timelines; as major conglomerates exit or reduce scale in this segment, established specialists like Hankook Steel gain structural importance. The shift toward premium vessel types—LNG carriers, eco-friendly ships, and offshore platforms—further supports demand for high-value cast components. The headquarters and main production facilities are located in Haman and Uiryeong, South Gyeongsang Province.
