Business

SY is a KOSDAQ-listed comprehensive building-materials company founded in 2000, with sandwich-panel manufacturing and sales as its core business. Key products — EPS, urethane, glass-wool (G/W), and flame-retardant EPS panels — serve roofing, exterior-wall, and interior applications, and SY holds the No. 1 domestic market share as the only listed company in the sector. The group has vertically integrated its supply chain through SY Build (color-coated steel sheets), SY Steeltech (deck plates, separately KOSDAQ-listed), and, following 2025 mergers, the formerly independent SY Chemical and SY Corning for upstream raw-material supply. Fourteen domestic production sites and twelve group entities — including five overseas entities in Vietnam (SY PANEL VINA, SY STEEL VINA), Cambodia (KENCANA SY BUILD), and Nepal (SY INDUSTRY) — enable an end-to-end, one-stop service covering materials through installation. International operations target Korean manufacturers expanding Southeast Asian production capacity and local infrastructure demand. New ventures include a rooftop solar business under SY Green Energy (est. 2024), structural insulated panels (SIPs) for modular housing via SY Build, a UPVC window-profile factory in Nepal's Chitwan (groundbreaking April 2026), and a cosmetics subsidiary — collectively aimed at broadening the revenue base beyond traditional construction materials.

Earnings

FY2022 delivered consolidated revenue of KRW 537.2bn and operating profit of KRW 14.0bn (OPM 2.6%), with owner-attributable net income of KRW 8.1bn. FY2023 maintained operating profit of KRW 13.6bn (OPM 2.4%), but an expansion of non-operating losses produced an owner-attributable net loss of KRW 9.0bn. FY2024 was a standout year: revenue reached a record KRW 561.0bn (+1.3% YoY), operating profit surged to KRW 21.9bn (OPM 3.9%), and the group returned to overall profitability with owner-attributable net income of KRW 3.4bn. FY2025, however, reversed sharply — revenue fell 10.5% to KRW 501.6bn as domestic construction investment contracted, industrial capex weakened, and foreign-exchange valuation losses widened. With the fixed-cost base largely unchanged, operations swung to an operating loss of KRW 6.6bn (OPM –1.3%), and owner-attributable net loss reached KRW 14.1bn. Operating cash flow deteriorated from +KRW 7.8bn in 2024 to –KRW 15.3bn in 2025. Quarterly, the FY2025 operating loss was KRW 2.0bn in Q1, narrowed to just KRW 0.09bn in Q2, then widened to KRW 0.95bn in Q3 and KRW 3.6bn in Q4, as second-half cost pressure intensified. Despite the net loss, total equity expanded significantly in 2025 (debt ratio improving from 121.3% to 93.2%), reflecting apparent capital inflows that offset loss-driven equity erosion. Q1 2026 showed early improvement: revenue of KRW 126.3bn rose 8.6% YoY (vs KRW 116.3bn in Q1 2025), the operating loss narrowed ~40% to KRW 1.2bn, and owner-attributable net loss improved modestly to KRW 3.4bn from KRW 3.7bn — pointing to gradual but incomplete recovery.
