Business

Cheryong Industrial was spun off from Cheryong Electric Co., Ltd. in 2011 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2012. Its two segments are metal products (hardware fittings) and synthetic-resin products, with metal fittings estimated to account for over 80% of total revenue. Core products include transmission fittings for 154kV, 345kV, 765kV, and 500kV HVDC lines, plus fittings for distribution, communications, and railway overhead lines; the resin segment covers underground cable protection plates and low-voltage junction boxes. Key customers are KEPCO and the Korea Electrical Manufacturers Cooperative, anchoring the company in Korea's public power-infrastructure market. A made-to-order, multi-product/small-batch production model creates specification-specific barriers to entry. The 500kV HVDC insulators, spacer dampers, and aluminum pipe jumper assemblies—completed in 2024—represent the highest voltage class of cross-linked polyethylene HVDC hardware domestically developed, conferring a de-facto sole-supplier role in the East-Coast-to-Seoul backbone grid project. The company is also collaborating with KEPCO KDN on new HVDC tower and OPGW fittings. The controlling shareholder is CEO Park Jong-tae and related parties at approximately 46.8%.
