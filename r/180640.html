As a holding company its own activities span hotels, online information services, and travel, but its core income comes from subsidiary brand royalties and dividends. With consolidated revenue around 290–300 billion won—tiny next to Korean Air's tens of trillions—Hanjin Kal's results are effectively tied to affiliate earnings and dividend policy.
