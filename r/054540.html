Business

Samyoung M-Tek, established in 1997 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2001, is a specialized manufacturer of industrial structural components and plant equipment, operating its main and second factories in Haman, South Gyeongsang Province, along with a joint-venture subsidiary DSD (Dalian Samyoung Doosan Metal Products) co-established with Hanwha Engine in Dalian, China. Its flagship product, MBS (Main Bearing Support), is a core structural component assembled into the engine blocks of large marine and power-generation diesel engines to support the crankshaft's main bearings; it has been designated a World-Class Product and holds approximately 58% of the global market. South Korea's large marine diesel engine market is dominated by HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, Hanwha Engine, and STX Engine, and Samyoung M-Tek supplies MBS to all three, securing a mission-critical position in the domestic shipbuilding supply chain. The Pallet Car — an essential piece of equipment for iron ore pelletizing processes — is produced exclusively in Korea and exported to India, China, and other markets, making it a core cash-generating product. The structural components division produces bridge bearings, elastomeric bearings, and seismic isolation dampers for bridge construction, while the wind energy division supplies high-value gearbox components. Key customers include major Korean conglomerates such as POSCO E&C, Doosan Enerbility, and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, alongside overseas clients like Germany's METSO and Nippon Steel of Japan, with growing contributions from Middle Eastern clients including Saudi Aramco and Qatari entities. The consolidation of SY Dong-A — a manufacturer of rubber components for the automotive and home appliance industries — has significantly diversified the group's portfolio beyond its steel-based heritage. Nuclear plant equipment components such as pumps and valves are also produced, a segment still small in revenue terms but positioned as a potential growth option if domestic and overseas nuclear construction programs accelerate. The company underpins its competitive strategy with a broad portfolio of patents and design rights, prioritizing production technology advancement and high-value-added product development.

Q1 2026 saw a sharp revenue surge to KRW 130.9 billion following SY Dong-A's consolidation, but operating income came in at KRW 5.61 billion (OPM 4.3%), reflecting the lower-margin rubber parts business now included in consolidated figures; controlling-interest net income improved markedly to KRW 3.80 billion from Q4 2025.
