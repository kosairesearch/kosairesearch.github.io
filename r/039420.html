Business

Founded in 1994 under the Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries through a public–private consortium, KL-Net is Korea's pioneering electronic data interchange (EDI) specialist for maritime logistics, listed on KOSDAQ since 2002. The business rests on four pillars: EDI-based electronic logistics services (B2G and B2B), system integration (SI), solution sales, and IT outsourcing. The B2G segment operates the government's Single Window, connecting shipping lines, freight forwarders, carriers, and cargo depots with port authorities, customs, immigration, hazardous-goods inspectors, and rail agencies—effectively digitizing the entire import/export licensing process under a government mandate. The flagship B2B product, PLISM (Port Logistics Information Service Management), processes booking, waybills, shipping documents, and cargo inspections among shippers, shipping lines, forwarders, carriers, and terminals via EDI, reducing logistics costs and strengthening port competitiveness. Newer offerings include PLISM ARA, LogisView, a global cargo-tracking service, and eTrans-W; KL-Net also developed the world's first digital-twin port logistics platform for Busan Port, enabling real-time visual monitoring of port operations. In autonomous vessel technology, KL-Net participates in government-funded R&D to develop real-time performance monitoring and fault-prediction systems for vessel machinery, and it previously joined a maritime logistics blockchain consortium with Samsung SDS, Korea Customs Service, the Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries, and IBM Korea. The SI and solutions segment centers on container terminal management systems such as ATOMS, where competition with large IT conglomerates is unavoidable. With approximately 180 employees, KL-Net maintains its head office in Gangnam, Seoul and regional offices in Busan and Sejong; the controlling shareholder group led by CEO Jeong Ji-won holds approximately 28.1%.
