Business

Hydro Lithium (formerly Korea SE) was founded in 1995 as a civil engineering materials manufacturer and listed on KOSDAQ in 2008. Following its acquisition by Lithium Plus in October 2022, the company was renamed and pivoted to secondary battery materials, completing the takeover of Lithium Plus's Geumsan, Chungnam lithium hydroxide plant and equipment in 2024. Its primary business is now the manufacturing and sale of ultra-high-purity battery-grade lithium hydroxide, lithium carbonate, and lithium chloride at the Geumsan plant; legacy civil engineering products (permanent anchors, PAP wall reinforcement, bridge cables) continue at a minor scale. CEO Dr. Jeon Woong's proprietary CULX eco-friendly brine lithium extraction technology is the company's core IP — its low-carbonate, high-purity lithium hydroxide targets NCM high-nickel cathode applications for electric vehicles. In 2025, the company independently developed 99.9%-purity lithium chloride for solid-state battery sulfide electrolyte applications and completed lithium bromide synthesis, broadening its specialty lithium chemicals portfolio. Hydro Lithium has secured land at Saemangeum National Industrial Complex for future mass-production capacity, and is pursuing long-term vertical integration through Bolivia's seven-salt-lake brine project and a Memorandum of Intent with Nevada Lithium for the Bonnie Claire project in the United States.
