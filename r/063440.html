Business

SM Life Design Group, established in 1998 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2002, is an integrated media infrastructure company within the SM Entertainment group operating across three formal segments: commercial printing, logistics operations, and studio space rental. The printing segment covers all physical K-POP printed products—albums, photobooks, posters, photocards, smart albums (SMini), and season greetings—with Hearts2Hearts, RIIZE, aespa, EXO, and NCT as core clients. A new contract with Starship Entertainment (IVE, CRAVITY, etc.) has begun flowing through from 2025, gradually diluting SM-affiliated IP concentration. The logistics segment manages approximately 10,000 SKUs and 1.4 million units of inventory via a WMS-based system in a 1,792 sqm warehouse at the Paju headquarters, earning fee-based consignment revenue closely correlated with SM Entertainment's standalone MD performance. The studio segment is anchored by the Paju SM Studio completed in late 2024, comprising 1,806 pyeong of floor area (three studio buildings, a carpentry workshop, and storage) across roughly 3,000 pyeong of land, designed to serve MV, advertising, and rehearsal filming demand. Subsidiary Moa L&B International handles wine import and distribution under the 'emos' brand as a diversification initiative. In-house printing equipment and self-operated logistics are central to profitability defense, and the infrastructure is scalable to include third-party logistics (3PL) services.

Earnings

Operating profit improved every year: ₩2.5bn (OPM 5.0%) in 2022, ₩2.7bn (5.7%) in 2023, ₩3.5bn (7.9%) in 2024, and ₩4.6bn (8.6%) in 2025, while parent-attributable net profit more than doubled from ₩2.5bn in 2022 to ₩5.5bn in 2025.
