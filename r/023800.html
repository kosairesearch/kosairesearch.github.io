Business

INZI Controls, founded in 1978 and headquartered in Siheung, Gyeonggi Province, has been listed on KOSPI since 1997. Its core business spans three pillars: ① Thermal Management Systems (TMS)—coolant control valves, thermostats, water pumps; ② Engine Management Sensors (EMS)—temperature sensors, knock sensors, fuel pressure regulators; and ③ Engineering Plastics—cylinder head covers, intake manifolds. The company has developed proprietary next-generation integrated coolant control valves applicable across ICE, EV, and hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, broadening its eco-friendly parts portfolio. It holds a leading domestic TMS market share, with Hyundai-Kia Group as the primary domestic OEM customer. Overseas production is based in China (Tianjin, Suzhou), India (Chennai), Hungary (Komárom), and the United States (Alabama); a German sales office opened in May 2024 to pursue European OEM customers. INZI is co-developing hydrogen fuel cell system coolant control components with GM and Honda, and participates as a joint research partner with Hanwha Aerospace in a fuel cell lightweighting project for aerial mobility. IATF 16949 certification and a USD 200M export award (2012) underpin the company's global OEM supply credentials, pointing to a long-term strategy of expanding its technical domain beyond conventional automotive into UAM and aerospace.
