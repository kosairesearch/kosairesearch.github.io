Business

Cenotech was founded in 1999 as the first domestic company to successfully mass-produce ceramic beads, and remains Korea's only specialist ceramic bead manufacturer. Its core business is grinding and dispersing ceramic beads, split between mining/paper/ink/paint applications and electronic-grade ultra-fine zirconia beads. As of Q1 2026, the revenue mix stands at roughly 48% mining and paper/ink, 18% electronics, 16% battery additives, 9% ceramic powder, and 9% other. Electronic-grade beads are critical consumables for MLCC raw-material nano-milling and EV battery cathode dispersion; Cenotech holds roughly a 9% share in that segment against two dominant Japanese producers with combined ~78%. In the general bead market for mining and paper, Cenotech has no domestic rivals and commands approximately 21% global share, supported by a network of regional distributors and warehouses across Europe, Africa, and the Americas. Flux (specialty welding material for shipbuilding), ceramic powder, and catalyst carriers round out the portfolio. A battery cathode additive division was launched in 2024, leveraging existing nano-powder processing technology and more than 70% of current production equipment to enter the segment capital-efficiently. The controlling shareholder is E&F Marble Holdings, a subsidiary of E&F Private Equity, an environment-focused fund. The company's stated long-term vision is to become a high-value composite materials specialist by 2030.

Earnings

Cenotech's four-year earnings record reflects an extreme cycle. In 2022, revenue was KRW 34.7 bn with operating profit of KRW 1.13 bn (OPM 3.2%), yet a minor net loss of KRW 0.07 bn persisted. 2023 was the trough: revenue KRW 31.1 bn, operating loss KRW 1.06 bn (OPM -3.4%), net loss KRW 5.35 bn. A partial operating recovery began in 2024 with revenue KRW 31.5 bn and operating profit KRW 0.95 bn (OPM 3.0%), but an elevated net loss of KRW 2.53 bn continued as heavy financial costs overwhelmed the operating improvement. FY2025 delivered a full turnaround: revenue KRW 35.6 bn (+13% YoY), operating profit KRW 3.17 bn (OPM 8.9%, +231% YoY), net profit KRW 1.35 bn. Management attributed the improvement to China-substitution demand for beads, rising mine utilization rates, and recovering shipbuilding flux orders. Quarterly, 2025 operating profit progressed from KRW 0.16 bn in Q1 to KRW 0.96 bn in Q2, KRW 0.73 bn in Q3, and KRW 1.32 bn in Q4; notably, Q4 net profit of KRW 2.02 bn exceeded operating profit, suggesting meaningful non-operating items in the quarter. Q1 2026 set a new quarterly revenue record at KRW 10.46 bn (+40% YoY), with battery additive quarterly revenue of KRW 1.6 bn already surpassing the full-year 2025 additive total of KRW 1.3 bn. Operating cash flow surging from KRW 1.42 bn (2024) to KRW 8.20 bn (2025) drove a meaningful deleveraging, with the debt-to-equity ratio declining from 161.1% in 2023 to 118.2% in 2025.

Industry

Ceramic beads are recurring consumable industrial materials used across mining, electronics, and battery manufacturing, providing a broad demand base that partially diversifies economic cycle exposure. Mining bead demand rose markedly in 2024-25 as ore hardness trends drove conversion to high-density beads and iron ore/gold mine utilization improved in Australia and South America. The electronics ceramic bead segment is dominated by two Japanese producers (~78% combined), limiting the pace of share gains for non-Japanese suppliers, though absolute market size continues to grow on MLCC and battery-electrode nano-milling demand. U.S.-China trade tensions have directly created substitution demand for non-Chinese suppliers; Cenotech benefits as a non-China source for customers seeking supply-chain diversification. The battery cathode additive market is growing alongside EV adoption and high-energy-density NCM trends; high-purity, uniformity, and process-control capabilities serve as key barriers to entry for transition-metal additives such as cobalt hydroxide. Shipbuilding sector recovery provides an incremental tailwind for flux sales. South Korea's government is actively building a domestic critical-mineral recycling ecosystem for rare earths and cobalt, which could open additional recycling-linked business opportunities for ceramics-process specialists.

Bear case

Limited Electronics Bead Market Share vs. Dominant Japanese Incumbents

Two Japanese incumbents control roughly 78% of the electronics-grade ultra-fine bead market, leaving Cenotech with a ~9% share. A persistent quality and brand recognition gap with Japanese leaders, combined with customers' conservative supplier-switching behavior, makes rapid share gains difficult. If penetration in this segment stalls, electronics division growth may be capped at the underlying market growth rate.

Intensifying Competition and Demand Uncertainty in Battery Additives

The battery cathode additive segment faces intense competition from Chinese low-cost suppliers and large established chemicals companies entering the space. Rising adoption of LFP batteries could shift demand away from NCM-type additives, while any deceleration in EV adoption would directly dampen end demand. Residual Leverage and Sensitivity to Mining Industry Cycles

The precedent of operating cash flow plunging to negative KRW 4.3 bn in 2022 underscores the need to continuously monitor whether the financial buffer is adequate if industry headwinds return.
