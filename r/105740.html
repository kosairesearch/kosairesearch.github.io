Business

DK-Lock is a KOSDAQ-listed instrumentation fitting (DK-LOK Fitting) and valve (D-PRO Valve) manufacturer founded in 1986 and listed in 2010, led by CEO Roh Eun-shik. Core products—tube fittings, ball valves, needle valves, and bellows valves used to transport, distribute, and control fluid and gas flows—are sold globally under the proprietary DK-Lok brand through an exclusive distributor network of approximately 92 agents across 46+ countries. The company serves roughly 300 clients including Exxon Mobil, DOW, and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, with exports accounting for approximately 76% of revenue. Revenue is anchored in oil & gas (approximately 55%), followed by semiconductors/displays (approximately 10%), with the remainder spread across shipbuilding/offshore, power generation, CNG/hydrogen, and other industrial uses. In aerospace/defense, DK-Lock holds a unique domestic position as Korea's sole aviation-grade fitting supplier, currently supplying KAI's KF-21 prototype with expansion to TA-50 and FA-50 platforms underway. In semiconductors, the company supplies UHP (Ultra High Purity) fittings and valves to Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix, while CNG/hydrogen fittings for fuel-cell buses and trucks represent a growing clean-energy segment. Competitively, the company benchmarks against global leader Swagelok, differentiating through equivalent technical quality with price competitiveness, and has secured international ship-classification certifications (ABS, DNV, BV, NK, KR) as a barrier to entry in shipbuilding.
