Business

GSE Co. was founded in 1989 as an IT networking firm but transformed into a pure-play city gas distributor following a 2010 merger with the original GSE utility and the discontinuation of its IT operations in 2011. The company purchases natural gas wholesale from Korea Gas Corporation (KOGAS) and distributes it via pipeline to retail customers in Jinju, Sacheon, and five counties in western Gyeongnam province (Hamyang, Geochang, Hadong, Sancheong, and Hapcheon), covering 48.3% of Gyeongnam's total land area. LNG and CNG sales together constitute approximately 99% of consolidated revenue, reflecting a near-single-business model with zero export exposure. Retail tariffs are set under a dual-regulation system: raw material costs are largely passed through from KOGAS wholesale rates, while the distributor's supply margin is separately approved by the Gyeongnam governor after a utility cost review. The customer mix spans residential, commercial, and industrial segments; the Jinju-Sacheon area hosts over 70% of Korea's aviation-related companies, including Korea Aerospace Industries (KAI), underpinning a solid industrial demand base. The company also operates CNG vehicle filling stations as part of the government's bus electrification initiative, though this contributes negligibly to revenue. GSE is the designated sole provider for pipeline maintenance and key facility installation across the Jinju municipal area, reinforcing its role as the backbone of regional energy infrastructure. It ranks in the lower-mid tier among Korea's 34 city gas distributors by supply volume, yet holds a legally protected franchise within its territory that structurally insulates it from direct competition.

Industry

Korea's city gas industry operates under a government-licensed natural monopoly framework in which 34 regional distributors each hold exclusive supply rights within designated zones, with no direct competition permitted within any single territory. The retail tariff structure is dually regulated: the wholesale gas price is approved by the central government, while each distributor's supply margin is reviewed and approved by the respective provincial governor after a formal cost hearing. The long-standing freeze on retail supply cost allowances has been a persistent source of margin pressure for regional distributors, creating structural inequities in tariff levels and profitability across the country. Korea relies entirely on seaborne LNG imports due to geographic constraints preventing pipeline gas access, leaving raw material costs exposed to international LNG benchmark prices and KRW exchange rate movements. Following the global energy market shock triggered by the 2022 Russia-Ukraine war, the subsequent price stabilization in 2024 limited domestic tariff increases and compressed the pass-through window for distributors. Domestic natural gas consumption is projected to grow 4.4% in 2025, with industrial and gas-manufacturing applications expected to lead demand. Over the medium to long term, building energy efficiency improvements, electrification trends including heat pump proliferation, and the gradual expansion of hydrogen fuel cell technology represent structural demand headwinds that the sector must navigate.
