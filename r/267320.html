Business

Naintech was established in 2007 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2020, specializing in battery and display manufacturing equipment. Its core products are lamination (bonding notched electrode sheets) and stacking (cell layering) equipment for lithium-ion battery cell manufacturing, supplied primarily to major battery cell makers such as LG Energy Solution through LG Electronics. In the display segment, the company supplies OLED manufacturing equipment and secured a KRW 25.4bn contract with LG Electronics in June 2026 for 8.6-generation inkjet OLED equipment, reaffirming its capabilities in the large-format OLED market. Subsidiary Thalos operates a military-grade large-capacity lithium-ion battery business, while subsidiary Energy11 is developing sodium-based batteries and solid-electrolyte-based ESS, with an ESS demonstration project ongoing with Korea East-West Power. New growth drivers include thermoelectric modules (Peltier-based precision cooling for AI data centers and semiconductors) and MXene (a 2D nanomaterial), alongside dry electrode processing equipment for solid-state batteries for which the company has secured a production-line delivery reference. A joint R&D program with Purdue University on MXene-based solid-state battery materials targets completion by 2028. The business model remains heavily order-driven across battery and display equipment, leaving it sensitive to customer capex cycles, while portfolio diversification into new segments is the central pillar of the medium-to-long-term strategy.
