Business

Toptec, founded in 1992 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2009, designs and manufactures factory automation equipment for the secondary battery, display, semiconductor, and smart factory sectors. Core product lines include EV battery module lines, tab welding systems, OLED/foldable/automotive display process equipment, unmanned logistics (AGV/OHT) systems, and hydrogen storage vessel automation lines. The FA segment drove 89.8% of consolidated Q1 2026 revenue, with product sales (9.9%) and other items (0.3%) making up the balance. The product sales segment is managed by subsidiary Lemon, which supplies high-performance nano-fiber membrane fabrics, filter media, and EMI shielding composite materials to the environmental and electronics markets. Major customers include SK On, Hyundai Mobis, and the Hyundai-SK On joint venture, with ongoing efforts to reduce the historical over-reliance on SK On. Collaboration with affiliated company Woo-Won Technology, a specialist in Z-stacking equipment, strengthens Toptec's turnkey capability across the battery assembly process. Overseas subsidiaries in the U.S., Hungary, Vietnam, and China support on-site installation and after-service. Key domestic competitors include Mplus and Hana Tech, with Toptec positioned as one of SK On's multi-vendor preferred suppliers for tab welding and logistics automation. In the longer term, the company is exploring diversification into defense and aerospace, and targeting the emerging AI-driven smart logistics center market.
