Business

Smotronic (formerly Woosu AMS) is a mobility drive solutions manufacturer founded in 1983 and listed on KOSDAQ in 2003. Its core competencies are aluminum die-casting and plastic injection molding, supplying four main product categories — transmission, engine, interior/exterior trim, and EV components — to global OEMs including Hyundai-Kia, Volkswagen, and Stellantis. Domestically, the company operates the Changwon factory (processing/assembly), Ulsan factory (processing/assembly), and two dedicated material plants for aluminum die-casting; overseas, it maintains a manufacturing subsidiary in India and a European logistics hub in the Czech Republic. Subsidiary Smotronic AMI handles plastic injection, painting, and deposition, while TMM focuses on micro-EV development and production. At the March 2026 AGM, the company rebranded from Woosu AMS to Smotronic to 'reflect the company's value and vision,' and in February 2026 the resignation of CEO Kim Sun-woo led to the appointment of sole CEO An Jung-gi. To address EV transition demand, the company has secured two large-format die-casting machines exceeding 2,000 tonnes — equipment compatible with Tesla's gigacasting and Hyundai's hypercasting approaches. Revenue remains heavily weighted toward domestic Hyundai-Kia, while the India subsidiary is positioned as a medium-term growth platform, although recent project execution disputes have elevated operational risk perceptions.
