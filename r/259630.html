Business

M Plus was founded in April 2003 in Cheongju, North Chungcheong Province, as a specialist in automated assembly-process equipment for secondary batteries. It is one of a handful of companies globally capable of supplying the full battery assembly process — notching, stacking, tab welding, packaging, and degassing — on a turnkey basis. The company holds proprietary technology for every major cell form factor including pouch, prismatic, and solid-state, while also developing cylindrical assembly equipment. Its customer base spans 23 global automakers (including GM) and 45 major battery manufacturers including LG Energy Solution and SK On; international credentials trace back to a 2008 partnership with A123 Systems in the U.S. The customer mix has diversified dramatically: SK On's revenue share dropped from 80%+ in 2022 to 22% in 2025, while 'other customers' expanded to 49%. Exports account for over 73% of revenue, with North American and European orders leading growth. The business is actively expanding into electrode-process equipment (coating, drying, calendering, slitting) and is developing face-press and roll-press equipment to resolve solid-state battery manufacturing bottlenecks. The 600 PPM ultra-high-speed notching machine — delivering more than twice the output of conventional systems — underpins high-margin orders, and R&D personnel account for approximately 60% of the workforce. The company has formally targeted KRW 700 billion in annual revenue by 2028, implying a 2024–2028 CAGR of approximately 52.7%.
