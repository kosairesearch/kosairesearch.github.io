Business

Following a change of controlling shareholder via a rights offering, the company rebranded as Soosung Webtoon in February 2024, formally pivoting from forklift manufacturing to webtoon and content. The primary driver of improvement has been the consolidation of webtoon platform Toomics and content studio Terapin, establishing a fully integrated content production-to-distribution chain. Toomics operates in 11 languages with overseas revenue exceeding 80% of the total; global membership stands at approximately 60 million, of which roughly 25 million are English-speaking. Soosung Webtoon expanded its stake in Toomics from 41% to 70.02%, bringing it fully into consolidation from October 2024. Subsidiary Toomics Global acquired a 96.14% stake in webtoon studio Terapin for KRW 29B, completing the deal in January 2025. Terapin holds 200+ titles across BL, romance-fantasy, and adult genres, and through linkage with Toomics platform it supplies original IP and adapts web-novel source material into webtoon exclusives, deepening vertical integration. Terapin already supplies content to more than ten major Japanese platforms including Line Manga, Piccoma, and Mechacomic. Soosung Webtoon's IP division is pursuing an OSMU (one source multi-use) strategy spanning romance, fantasy, martial arts, and BL genres. A legacy forklift-and-logistics business remains at the standalone entity level but is negligible in consolidated terms.

In 2024, Toomics' partial consolidation from Q4 expanded revenue to KRW 68.6B (+66% YoY) and narrowed the operating loss to roughly KRW 0.3B, though the controlling-interest net loss remained at KRW 8.9B. In 2025, full-year consolidation of Toomics and Terapin lifted revenue to KRW 92.8B (+35% YoY) and generated operating profit of KRW 5.2B (OPM 5.6%), ending four consecutive years of operating loss. Quarterly operating profit improved steadily through Q1 (KRW 2.9B), Q2 (KRW 3.1B), and Q3 (KRW 3.2B). The near-zero consolidated Q4 revenue (-KRW 0.2B) reflects consolidation eliminations or asset revaluation adjustments; impairment of goodwill and intangible assets tied to the Toomics/Terapin acquisitions is the most probable cause of the outsized net loss. Operating cash flow (CFO) came in at KRW 19.3B, confirming that core cash generation capacity remains intact. In 2026 Q1, revenue was KRW 22.6B and operating profit KRW 0.5B, a marked step-down from the ~KRW 3.1B quarterly average seen over 2025 Q1–Q3.
